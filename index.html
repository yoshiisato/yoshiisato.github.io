Research

In a world where data is the new currency, trust and privacy are no longer optional—they're foundational.

I'm driven by the question: How do we build cloud infrastructures that are not just fast and scalable, but fundamentally trustworthy?

At the intersection of hardware, software, and virtualization security lies a uniquely complex trust problem. My research addresses this through confidential computing and its real-world integration.

Current Research Projects

  1. GPU Throughput Evaluations for CVMs - Measuring GPU throughput overheads across different CVMs to understand I/O performance over different vendor HW.
    Keywords: Intel TDX, NVIDIA GPU, CUDA.
  2. Serverless Confidential Containers - Researching Kubernetes serverless deployments on confidential computing infrastructure to enable privacy-perserving serverless cloud.
    Keywords: K8s, Knative, Confidential Containers (CoCo), Kata Container, Intel TDX, AMD SEV-SNP.

Core Research Interests

  • Confidential Computing
  • Secure distributed systems using TEEs (e.g. anonymity networks, serverless)
  • Privacy-preserving MPC using TEEs (for bioinformatics, federated learning)
